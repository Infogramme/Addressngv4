02 — Objectives & Audience

02 — Objectives & Target Audience

Who we're building for, and what we need them to do

Primary objectives

  1. Establish narrative authority. Address Nigeria must be perceived as the definitive national addressing standard before competitors or appropriators can claim the frame.
  2. Generate partnership pipeline. Insurance, telco, and pension executives must be able to understand the value proposition in under 90 seconds and find a clear next step (demo request, NDA, pilot inquiry).
  3. Build public legitimacy. Citizens, media, and civil society stakeholders need to understand the concept and trust its federated, bottom-up design.
  4. Support diaspora channels. PROTIVA diaspora workflows and investment verification require an international-facing presence with global credibility signals.
  5. Enable WhatsApp amplification. Every page must be shareable — og:image, concise titles, open graph metadata — because WhatsApp is the primary distribution channel for Nigeria's decision-maker class.

Audience segments and site journeys

Private-sector executives

Insurance, pensions, telco, fintech. Seek: KYC uplift, fraud reduction, compliance efficiency. Journey: Homepage → Verify (CRYLID) → Partner with us.

Government & revenue institutions

FIRS, JTB, land registries, LGA admin. Seek: addressable tax base, fraud intelligence, title verification. Journey: Homepage → How it works → Get briefed.

Diaspora & property investors

Nigerians abroad seeking verified property investment. Journey: Homepage → PROTIVA/NINjaMap → Verify a property / Get started.

Media, policy & civil society

Journalists, think tanks, NGOs, academics. Seek: credible facts, downloadable brief, IP accountability narrative. Journey: About → Press kit → Report.

05 — Visual Identity

05 — Visual Identity Parameters

Typography, colour, and design language

The visual language must convey sovereign infrastructure — not startup flair. The reference point is closer to the Ordnance Survey, the Bank of England's digital estate, or the United Nations data platforms: authoritative, precise, enduring.

Typography system

Font rationale. Playfair Display gives the editorial weight of a founding document — appropriate for a sovereign infrastructure initiative. DM Sans provides functional clarity for interface copy. DM Mono handles the technical layer (address codes, API strings, coordinates) with precision.

Nigerian green anchors the palette as a national identity signal. Gold provides the gravitas of official documentation. Mint is used sparingly for interactive trust-layer moments (CRYLID marks, verification states). Parchment grounds the document-like reading experience.

Design principles

  1. Document-grade typography. Generous leading, editorial hierarchy. The site should feel like it was designed to last 20 years, not 20 months.
  2. Address codes as design elements. AiG-CTG codes are visually distinctive — monospaced, structured, uniquely Nigerian. They should appear as design motifs, not just data.
  3. Grid-as-metaphor. The AiG tessellation grid is the conceptual spine of the product. Subtle grid overlays, tilework patterns, and map-derived textures reinforce the spatial intelligence narrative.
  4. WhatsApp-first shareability. Every hero section must produce a compelling og:image (1200×630). Clean, large-type, minimal — renders well at 300px thumbnail width.
  5. No startup aesthetics. No floating blobs, no purple-to-pink gradients, no animated particle canvases. This is sovereign infrastructure for a nation of 220 million.
06 — Technical Stack

06 — Technical Stack Recommendation

Technology choices for a sovereign-grade web presence

Framework: Next.js 14+ (App Router)

Server-side rendering for SEO and WhatsApp crawlers. Static generation for content pages. Edge-ready for performance across Nigerian network conditions. TypeScript throughout.

CMS: Sanity.io (headless)

Content team can update press releases, module descriptions, and team pages without engineering involvement. Structured content + GROQ queries. Portable text for rich editorial blocks.

Maps: Mapbox GL JS

Custom-styled Nigeria basemap. AiG-CTG grid overlay rendered as vector tiles. NINjaMap teaser integration. Offline-capable tiles for poor-connectivity demos.

Forms & CRM: Tally + Pipedrive

Tally for embedded forms (partner inquiry, demo request). Webhook to Pipedrive for NDA-aware pipeline management. Zero-dependency, GDPR-compliant form handling.

Analytics: Plausible (privacy-first)

No cookies, GDPR-compliant. Nigeria-aware traffic intelligence. Appropriate for a sovereign data infrastructure brand — no Google Analytics tracking irony.

Hosting: Vercel + Cloudflare

Vercel for Next.js deployment with edge CDN. Cloudflare for DNS, DDoS protection, and Nigerian PoP acceleration (Lagos edge node). Aligns with DG-VS-SiS-774 security posture.

Security baseline. All form submissions over HTTPS with HSTS preloading. Content Security Policy headers. No third-party analytics scripts beyond Plausible. Partner inquiry data is never stored on the public-facing infrastructure — routed immediately to a Pipedrive-controlled environment with NDA flags. Aligns with DG-VS-SiS-774 data exfiltration risk profile.
